# 01 — Game Identity

---

## Game Title

### Ember Tide

The name carries a dual meaning rooted in the game's lore. **Ember** refers to the fading Hearthstones — ancient crystals that once blazed with protective fire but now glow as faint embers on the verge of dying out. **Tide** refers to the Hollow Tide, the wave of sentient darkness that floods the world wherever the light retreats. Together, "Ember Tide" describes the central tension of the game: the last flickers of warmth pushing back against an ocean of shadow. It also describes the protagonist Solen herself — she is the ember, small and fragile, standing against a tide that has already swallowed nearly everything.

In the world of Lumara, the phrase "ember tide" is an old lamplighter expression meaning "the hour before the flame dies," used to describe the final window of opportunity to save a lantern before it goes out forever. Solen's grandmother used to say it when handing her the matchbox at dusk: *"It's the ember tide, girl — light them quick or the dark eats the road."*

---

## Tagline

*Rekindle the light before the world forgets it ever shone.*

---

## Genre & Subgenre

| Category | Detail |
|---|---|
| Primary Genre | Action-Platformer |
| Subgenre | Metroidvania-lite / Narrative Platformer |
| Scene Type | GB Studio PLATFORMER |
| Perspective | Side-scrolling 2D |

The game is primarily a linear action-platformer with tight jump-and-run gameplay, but it borrows from the Metroidvania tradition by gating certain areas behind ability upgrades (wall jump, dash, double jump) and encouraging backtracking to earlier worlds to find secrets once new abilities are acquired. The "lite" qualifier is important: the critical path is always clear and the game never requires backtracking to progress through the main story, but completionists who do backtrack are rewarded with lore, collectibles, and access to the true ending.

---

## Tone & Mood

Ember Tide balances **melancholy with determination**. The world of Lumara is beautiful but wounded — forests that used to glow with bioluminescent fungi are dimming, frozen lakes reflect a sky that hasn't seen stars in weeks, and villages sit half-abandoned with lanterns still burning in empty windows. The tone is not grimdark; it is wistful, like autumn light through stained glass. There is sadness, but it is always tempered by warmth. Solen cracks jokes when she's scared. NPCs share memories of better days alongside practical survival advice. Even the enemies — corrupted animals and shadow-taken villagers — evoke pity as much as threat.

The mood shifts across the five worlds:

| World | Mood |
|---|---|
| Verdant Hollow (Forest) | Nostalgic, gentle, faintly ominous — the calm before the storm |
| Frostfang Peaks (Ice/Mountain) | Isolated, harsh, quietly beautiful — endurance against cold |
| Sunken Brine (Underwater Ruins) | Eerie, mysterious, oppressive — ancient secrets and pressure |
| Cinderforge Depths (Volcanic Caverns) | Intense, dangerous, defiant — fire fighting back against dark |
| The Spire Ascent (Sky Citadel) | Epic, desperate, bittersweet — the final climb toward truth |

The game's emotional arc follows a structure of **loss → resolve → discovery → doubt → sacrifice → hope**. The player should feel, by the end, that the journey mattered — not because of what was gained, but because of what was preserved.

## Inspirations

| Game | Platform | What It Inspires |
|---|---|---|
| **Kirby's Dream Land 2** | Game Boy | Tight, forgiving platforming feel; the way levels flow from left to right with satisfying rhythm. Ember Tide aims for this kind of accessible-but-deep movement. |
| **The Legend of Zelda: Link's Awakening DX** | GBC | Narrative depth on limited hardware. The way Link's Awakening told a melancholy, philosophical story through small dialogue boxes and environmental storytelling is the gold standard Ember Tide aspires to. |
| **Shantae** (2002) | GBC | Vibrant GBC color palettes, expressive character animation in limited sprite sizes, and the Metroidvania-lite structure of unlocking abilities that open earlier areas. |
| **Mega Man V** | Game Boy | Boss design philosophy — each boss has a pattern, a weakness, and a personality. The boss-rush structure and tight combat windows inform Ember Tide's boss encounters. |
| **Donkey Kong Land III** | GBC | Environmental variety and the way it pushed the hardware to deliver distinct world themes (jungle, water, factory, snow) while keeping a consistent art style. |
| **Gargoyle's Quest** | Game Boy | A platformer with strong story and RPG elements woven in. The way that game gave weight to its world through NPC dialogue and lore tablets directly inspires Ember Tide's approach. |
| **Celeste** (modern, not GBC) | Multi | Not a GBC game, but its philosophy of "hard platforming with a compassionate story about perseverance" is the emotional template for Ember Tide. Solen's journey mirrors Madeline's: the mountain is inside as much as outside. |

## Target Audience

| Audience Segment | Detail |
|---|---|
| Primary | Retro gaming enthusiasts aged 18–35 who grew up with or appreciate Game Boy / GBC era games |
| Secondary | GB Studio community members and homebrew developers looking for a polished reference project |
| Tertiary | Indie game fans who enjoy narrative-driven platformers (Celeste, Shovel Knight, Hollow Knight) and would appreciate a GBC-style take on the genre |
| Accessibility | The game is designed to be completable by players of moderate skill; difficulty comes from optional content and the true ending path, not the critical path |

The game assumes familiarity with D-pad + A/B button controls and side-scrolling platformer conventions. No tutorial is needed beyond the first scene's environmental teaching (the classic "pit that teaches you to jump" approach). Dialogue can be advanced or skipped for players who want pure gameplay.

## Estimated Playtime

| Path | Estimated Time |
|---|---|
| Main story (critical path only) | 3–4 hours |
| Main story + side content | 5–6 hours |
| 100% completion (all collectibles, secrets, true ending) | 7–8 hours |
| Speedrun potential (any%) | 25–40 minutes |

The game is designed to be completable in a few focused sessions, respecting the handheld tradition of pick-up-and-play. Checkpoint placement is generous on the critical path. The longer playtime for completionists comes from hidden rooms, backtracking with new abilities, lore collection, and the requirements for the true ending (collecting all 25 Ember Shards and all 5 Lore Stones).
