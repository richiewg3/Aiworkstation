## Ember Shards — Full List

Ember Shards are orange crystalline fragments that glow when collected. They are hidden throughout every world — some in plain sight but requiring platforming skill, others in secret areas requiring specific abilities.

### Prologue: Cinderhearth (1 Shard)

| # | Scene | Location | How to Access |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0-1 | Behind Maren's house, on the roof | Jump from the well platform to the roof edge. Requires precise timing and knowledge of the jump height. Easy to miss on first playthrough. |

## Lore Stones — Full List

Lore Stones contain fragments of the First Keeper incantation needed to activate the Lantern of Origin (true ending). Each is a blue rune stone that triggers a dialogue event when collected, showing the incantation fragment.

| # | World | Scene | Location | Incantation Fragment |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1 | 1-3 | Side alcove in the Thornveil Thicket shaft | "By the light that was first kindled..." |
| 2 | 2 | 2-4 | Behind the summit shrine exterior wall | "...and the dark that was first sealed..." |
| 3 | 3 | 3-3 | Inside Varek's dismantled First Keeper device | "...we offer this flame as key and door..." |
| 4 | 4 | 4-3 | Behind a cracked obsidian formation (different from Shard #19) | "...to sever the cord between worlds..." |
| 5 | 5 | 5-3 | In a hidden room behind a cracked stained-glass window (same room as Shard #25) | "...and grant the light its own dominion." |

**Complete Incantation:**
"By the light that was first kindled, and the dark that was first sealed, we offer this flame as key and door, to sever the cord between worlds, and grant the light its own dominion."

## Secret Ending Trigger Conditions

To unlock the true ending, the player must meet ALL of the following conditions before defeating Varek:

| Condition | Requirement | Tracking |
|---|---|---|
| All Ember Shards | 25 / 25 collected | Visible in inventory |
| All Lore Stones | 5 / 5 collected | Visible in inventory; incantation assembles automatically |
| Defeat Varek | Complete the final boss fight | Story progression |
| Chamber of Origin | After defeating Varek, the stairway opens if both shard and stone conditions are met | Automatic — the floor opens if conditions are satisfied |

**What Happens If Conditions Are Not Met:**
If the player defeats Varek without all 25 Ember Shards and all 5 Lore Stones, the normal ending plays. The stairway to the Chamber of Origin does not appear. The player can reload their save (which returns them to the pre-Varek checkpoint) and collect the remaining items before fighting Varek again.

**How to Know Progress:**
The pause menu inventory screen shows:
- Ember Shards: X / 25
- Lore Stones: X / 5

When all 5 Lore Stones are collected, the inventory displays the complete incantation. When all 25 Ember Shards are collected, the inventory shows a note: "The shards resonate. Something ancient stirs beneath the Spire."

## Easter Eggs

### Easter Egg 1: The Lamplighter's Song

**Location:** Cinderhearth, Scene 0-1 (revisit after clearing World 2)

**Trigger:** Return to Cinderhearth and interact with the well 7 times.

**Result:** On the 7th interaction, instead of the normal "It's a well" text, Solen hums a melody: "Hmm hmm hm-hm hmm..." followed by text: "That's the old Lamplighter's Song. Mother used to hum it on the long roads. I didn't realize I still remembered it." This melody is the main motif from Track 01 — "The Lamplighter's Road."

---

### Easter Egg 2: Fern's Mushroom Encyclopedia

**Location:** World 1, Scene 1-2 (revisit after clearing World 3)

**Trigger:** Return to the Mushroom Grotto after the Brine Hearthstone is reignited. Interact with Fern 5 times in succession.

**Result:** After the 5th interaction, Fern launches into an impassioned monologue about mushroom classification: "Did you know there are over forty species of bioluminescent fungus in the Verdant Hollow alone? Forty! And that's just what I've catalogued. The Glow-cap Cluster alone has seven subspecies. Seven! I could talk about this for hours." Solen responds: "Please don't." Fern: "Too late. Subspecies one: the Common Glow-cap..."  The dialogue box fills with increasingly specific mushroom taxonomy for 8 text boxes before Solen interrupts: "Fern. Fern. FERN. I have to go save the world." Fern: "Right. Sorry. But when you're done, I'm showing you my field sketches."

---

### Easter Egg 3: Wrench's Turret Revenge

**Location:** World 4, Scene 4-1 (revisit after clearing World 4)

**Trigger:** Return to Wrench's maintenance bay after the Forge Hearthstone is reignited.

**Result:** Wrench has rebuilt his turrets. If the player walks into the corridor where the turrets were empty before, one fires a harmless puff of steam at Solen. Wrench shouts from off-screen: "WORKING! THEY'RE WORKING THIS TIME!" Solen: "Wrench, that one just shot steam at me." Wrench: "CALIBRATION IN PROGRESS!"

---

### Easter Egg 4: The Developer's Message

**Location:** Chamber of Origin, Scene 6-1

**Trigger:** After acquiring the Lantern of Origin but before activating it (choosing NO on the dialogue choice), inspect the far-right wall of the chamber.

**Result:** A tiny inscription barely visible on the wall reads: "Built with love, lantern light, and far too much coffee. — The Maker." This is a developer self-insert that references the real process of making the game.

---

### Easter Egg 5: Orin's Regret

**Location:** World 2, Scene 2-1 (revisit after clearing World 5 — post-game)

**Trigger:** Return to Orin's cabin after defeating Varek.

**Result:** Orin is standing outside his cabin instead of inside. His dialogue is completely different: "You did it. I felt it — the Hearthstone blazed so bright I could see it from here. All five. You know, when I was young, I thought I'd be the one to walk the full road. Instead, I ran away and let a nineteen-year-old do it." A pause. "I'm going back to Cinderhearth. It's time I stopped hiding." If the player has triggered the true ending, Orin adds: "The stars... I've never seen them before. None of us have. What a thing. What a beautiful, terrifying thing."

---

### Easter Egg 6: The Hidden Melody

**Location:** World 3, Scene 3-4 (Keeper Cathedral)

**Trigger:** In the Keeper Cathedral, there are 5 seaweed strands that serve as climbable ladders. If the player climbs each one in order from left to right without touching the ground between them, a hidden melody plays — 5 ascending notes that form the first phrase of the main motif from Track 01.

**Result:** After the melody plays, a text box appears: "The cathedral remembers. The Keepers built music into the walls. This is the oldest song in Lumara." A small visual sparkle effect appears on each seaweed strand.
