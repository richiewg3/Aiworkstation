# 02 — Story & Narrative

---

## The World of Lumara

### Geography and History

Lumara is a small, fractured world — not a planet in the cosmic sense, but a landmass suspended in a vast, dark void called the Abyssal Sea. Nobody knows what lies beyond the Abyssal Sea; ships that sail too far simply never return. Lumara itself is roughly the size of a large island chain, connected by stone bridges, underground tunnels, and — in one case — a chain of floating rock platforms held aloft by ancient magic.

The world is divided into five distinct regions, each shaped by one of the five Hearthstones — massive crystals the size of houses that were planted into the earth by a civilization known as the First Keepers, long before recorded history. The Hearthstones radiate warmth, light, and life force. They are why forests grow, why rivers flow, why the air is breathable. Each Hearthstone tunes the environment around it: the Verdant Hearthstone feeds the forests and meadows, the Frost Hearthstone stabilizes the mountain climate into crisp cold rather than killing blizzards, the Brine Hearthstone keeps the underground seas navigable and lit, the Forge Hearthstone channels volcanic energy safely into the caverns below, and the Spire Hearthstone holds the sky citadel aloft and keeps the upper winds calm enough to breathe.

For centuries, the Lamplighter Order maintained the Hearthstones. They were not warriors or scholars but caretakers — ordinary people who carried lanterns fueled by Hearthstone light and walked the roads at dusk to keep the darkness at bay. The Hollow Tide — a creeping, living darkness that seeps from the Abyssal Sea — is always pressing against Lumara's borders, and the Hearthstones are the only thing that repels it. When a Hearthstone burns bright, the Hollow Tide cannot enter its region. When a Hearthstone dims, shadows creep in. When a Hearthstone goes dark, the Hollow Tide floods the land, corrupting creatures, petrifying people, and eventually dissolving everything into nothing.

### The Current State of Lumara

Twelve years before the game begins, a man named Varek — the most respected Keeper of the Lamplighter Order — made a discovery in the ruins beneath the Sunken Brine. He found a chamber built by the First Keepers that contained a warning: the Hearthstones were not gifts. They were locks. The First Keepers had used them to seal away the Abyssal Sea's true nature — not mere darkness, but a living entity called the Hollow, an ancient consciousness that predated Lumara itself. The Hearthstones didn't just repel the dark; they imprisoned it.

Varek became obsessed with this knowledge. He came to believe that the Hollow was not evil but simply a natural force that the First Keepers had wrongfully caged. He believed that by extinguishing the Hearthstones and allowing the Hollow to reclaim Lumara, he could broker a peace between light and dark — a new equilibrium where humanity would evolve to live in shadow rather than depending on fragile crystals that could fail at any time. He saw himself not as a destroyer but as a liberator.

Over the span of a decade, Varek — now calling himself the Hollow King — gathered followers, corrupted key members of the Lamplighter Order, and systematically sabotaged the Hearthstones. One by one, they dimmed. The Verdant Hearthstone was the first to flicker. The Frost Hearthstone followed. The world grew darker year by year, so gradually that most people didn't realize what was happening until it was too late.

Three months before the game begins, Varek extinguished the Forge Hearthstone outright — the first to go fully dark. The Hollow Tide surged into the Cinderforge Depths, corrupting everything it touched. Creatures became shadow-twisted. Miners were petrified. The news spread across Lumara like a shockwave.

Now, at the start of the game, four of the five Hearthstones are critically dim, and the fifth — the Spire Hearthstone in the sky citadel — is under direct siege by Varek and his shadow forces. Villages are barricading their doors. The Lamplighter Order has scattered. The roads are dark. And in the small village of Cinderhearth, at the base of the Verdant Hollow, a young lamplighter's apprentice named Solen watches her mother turn to stone before her eyes.

### What Makes Lumara Feel Alive

Despite the encroaching darkness, Lumara is not a dead world. It is a world in the process of dying, which is different — and more heartbreaking. Flowers still bloom in patches of Hearthstone light. Animals huddle near lanterns. NPCs tell stories about festivals they used to hold, recipes they used to cook, songs they used to sing. There are children's drawings scratched into walls, laundry still hanging on lines in abandoned villages, half-finished meals on tables in empty houses. The world feels like someone just stepped out of the room and never came back. This quality — life interrupted — is what makes the player want to save it.

## The Protagonist: Solen

Solen is nineteen years old, the daughter of Maren (a senior Lamplighter) and Edric (a carpenter who died in a mine collapse when Solen was seven). She grew up in Cinderhearth, the last village in the Verdant Hollow that still has working lanterns. She is a Lamplighter's apprentice — not a full Lamplighter, not a warrior, not a chosen one. She is simply the person who was standing in the right place at the wrong time, holding her mother's lantern when everything went dark.

### Why Solen Specifically

Solen's motivation is not destiny. It is grief and love. When the Hollow Tide surges into Cinderhearth during the opening scene, Maren pushes Solen out of the way of a wave of shadow energy and is struck herself. The petrification is not instant; it creeps up Maren's body over the course of thirty seconds while Solen watches, unable to do anything. Maren's last words before her mouth turns to stone are: *"The lantern... keep it lit... find the Hearthstones..."*

Solen does not set out to save the world. She sets out to save her mother. The fact that saving her mother requires saving the world is, from Solen's perspective, an inconvenient side effect. This is what makes her relatable and what makes her different from a generic hero: her scale is personal. She would walk through the Hollow itself for one person. The world just happens to be in the way.

The lantern staff Solen carries is her mother's — a tall walking stick with a brass lantern at the top, fueled by a shard of Hearthstone crystal. It is both her weapon (she swings it like a staff) and her light source. As she rekindles each Hearthstone throughout the game, the lantern burns brighter, and her attack power and reach increase. The lantern is a physical representation of hope: when it burns bright, the shadows recede; when it flickers, the darkness closes in.

## The Inciting Incident

The game opens in Cinderhearth on an ordinary evening. Solen is making her rounds, lighting the village lanterns along the main road — this serves as the movement tutorial. The sky is dim (it has been dim for weeks) but the village is warm and safe.

As Solen lights the last lantern at the village gate, the ground trembles. A sound like tearing fabric fills the air. The Verdant Hearthstone — visible on a hill in the background — flickers violently and dims to a faint pulse. The Hollow Tide surges through the valley in a visible wave of black-purple shadow. Villagers scream. Lanterns shatter.

Solen runs toward her home. She finds her mother Maren standing in the doorway, lantern staff in hand, holding back a tendril of shadow. Maren sees Solen and shoves the lantern staff into her hands. The shadow strikes Maren. The petrification begins at her feet and climbs. Solen reaches for her, but Maren pulls her hand away — she doesn't want Solen to be touching her when the stone takes hold, because physical contact during petrification can spread it.

Maren speaks her final words. The stone claims her. Solen stands alone in the doorway, holding a lantern that is the only light left in Cinderhearth.

The village elder, Brennan, finds Solen and tells her the truth: the only way to reverse the petrification is to reignite all five Hearthstones to full power. The combined light will purge the Hollow Tide and restore everyone it has taken. But no one has successfully traveled between the Hearthstones in months — the roads are overrun. Brennan gives Solen a map and a choice. Solen does not hesitate.

## Full Story — Beat by Beat

### Act I: The Verdant Hollow (World 1)

Solen leaves Cinderhearth and enters the Verdant Hollow, a once-lush forest now half-consumed by shadow. The trees still stand but their leaves are ashen. Bioluminescent mushrooms that once lit the forest floor have gone dark except in small clusters near the path. Solen encounters her first corrupted creatures — shadow-twisted rabbits and foxes that attack on sight but seem more frightened than aggressive.

As she moves deeper into the forest, Solen meets **Fern**, a young herbalist who was gathering medicinal plants when the Hollow Tide surged. Fern has been surviving alone in a tree hollow for days. She gives Solen a healing potion recipe and tells her about a hidden path through the Thornveil Thicket — a dense, bramble-choked area that the shadows haven't fully penetrated because the thorns are too thick even for darkness.

Solen navigates the Thornveil Thicket (the game's first mechanical challenge area, introducing wall jumping) and reaches the Verdant Hearthstone chamber. The Hearthstone is dim but not dead. Guarding it is the **Briar Sentinel**, a massive plant creature that was once the Hearthstone's natural guardian but has been corrupted by the Hollow Tide. It attacks Solen not out of malice but because it can no longer distinguish friend from foe.

After defeating the Briar Sentinel, Solen touches her lantern to the Hearthstone. The crystal blazes back to life. Light floods the Verdant Hollow. The corrupted creatures revert. The forest begins to bloom again. Solen's lantern burns brighter — she can now see in darker areas and her attack deals more damage.

**Story Beat 1 — A Loss:** The victory is bittersweet. As the light returns, Solen sees dozens of petrified villagers throughout the forest — people she knew, people who didn't make it to shelter. The Hearthstone's light alone isn't enough to restore them. She needs all five. This is the moment Solen realizes the scale of what she's facing and nearly breaks down. Fern finds her sitting at the base of a petrified farmer and says: *"You can't carry them all at once. But you can carry the light to the next stone. And the next. That's how lamplighters work, isn't it? One lantern at a time."*

### Act II: Frostfang Peaks (World 2)

The path north leads to the Frostfang Peaks, a mountain range where the Frost Hearthstone once kept the climate in a stable, crisp winter — beautiful but survivable. Without the Hearthstone at full power, the peaks have become brutally cold. Snow blinds. Wind pushes Solen backward on exposed ledges. Ice platforms crack and fall.

Solen encounters **Orin**, a retired Lamplighter who lives in a cabin on the lower slopes. Orin is old, bitter, and has given up hope. He tells Solen that Varek was his apprentice — that he trained the man who would become the Hollow King. Orin blames himself for not seeing the signs. He refuses to help Solen directly but grudgingly gives her his old climbing gear (enabling the wall jump upgrade if not already acquired, or improving its height).

Higher on the peaks, Solen discovers a frozen village — Rimehaven — where the inhabitants are not petrified but frozen in ice. The Hollow Tide interacts differently with the Frost Hearthstone's energy: instead of turning people to stone, it traps them in ice that will not melt. Solen finds a child frozen mid-step, reaching for a door handle. The image stays with her.

At the summit, the Frost Hearthstone sits in an open-air shrine battered by howling wind. The **Glacial Wyrm**, a serpentine ice dragon corrupted by the Hollow, coils around the Hearthstone. The fight takes place on a narrow mountain ledge with gusts of wind that push the player toward the edge.

After defeating the Glacial Wyrm, Solen reignites the Frost Hearthstone. The killing blizzard softens into gentle snowfall. The frozen villagers remain frozen — like the petrified ones, they need all five Hearthstones — but the ice stops spreading.

**Story Beat 2 — A Discovery:** After the Hearthstone is lit, Solen finds a hidden chamber behind the shrine containing a mural painted by the First Keepers. The mural depicts the five Hearthstones arranged in a star pattern, and in the center is a sixth light — something the current lore never mentions. Solen sketches it in her journal. She doesn't understand it yet, but it will become critical later: the sixth light is the Lantern of Origin, the key to the true ending.

### Act III: Sunken Brine (World 3)

Below the mountains lies the Sunken Brine — a network of underground seas, flooded ruins, and bioluminescent caverns. This is where Varek first discovered the truth about the Hearthstones, and the ruins of the First Keepers are everywhere: carved walls, broken machinery, sealed doors with symbols that glow when Solen's lantern approaches.

Solen meets **Coral**, a diver and salvager who has been living in the ruins for years, long before the Hollow Tide. Coral is cheerful, reckless, and knows the underwater passages better than anyone alive. She agrees to guide Solen through the flooded sections in exchange for help recovering a personal item: a locket containing a portrait of her late partner, lost somewhere in the deep ruins.

The Sunken Brine introduces swimming and ladder mechanics. Water sections limit Solen's movement speed and jump height but allow her to reach areas otherwise inaccessible. The environment is hauntingly beautiful: shafts of green light filter through cracks in the cavern ceiling, schools of luminescent fish dart through flooded hallways, and ancient statues of the First Keepers stand with hands outstretched, as if offering something that was taken long ago.

Deep in the ruins, Solen discovers Varek's research chamber — the room where he first learned the truth about the Hollow. His journals are scattered across the desk. Reading them reveals his perspective: he genuinely believed he was doing the right thing. He writes about the Hollow with something close to reverence, describing it as "the original state of the world" and the Hearthstones as "chains of light imposed by fearful gods." Solen is disturbed by how reasonable he sounds.

The Brine Hearthstone sits at the bottom of a massive underwater cathedral. The **Abyssal Leviathan**, a kraken-like creature made of shadow and brine, guards it. The fight takes place partially underwater, requiring the player to manage air supply while dodging tentacle attacks.

After defeating the Leviathan and reigniting the Brine Hearthstone, the underground seas clear and light returns to the caverns.

**Story Beat 3 — A Betrayal:** As Solen exits the Hearthstone chamber, she is confronted by **Shade**, a figure in a dark cloak who has been following her since the Verdant Hollow. Shade reveals himself as a former Lamplighter — and one of Varek's agents. He tells Solen that Varek anticipated someone would try to relight the Hearthstones and has been allowing it to happen because each reignition sends a pulse of energy that Varek can redirect to power his assault on the Spire Hearthstone. Every Hearthstone Solen has reignited has made Varek stronger. Solen has been helping the enemy without knowing it. Shade offers her a choice: stop now, or continue and feed Varek more power. Then he vanishes. Solen is shaken to her core but presses on — she has no other option. Her mother is still stone.

### Act IV: Cinderforge Depths (World 4)

The Cinderforge Depths are the volcanic caverns beneath Lumara — a network of magma rivers, forge chambers, and mining tunnels. This was the first region to fall completely to the Hollow Tide, and it shows. The corruption here is total: lava has turned a sickly purple-black, metal has rusted into jagged shapes, and the air itself seems to resist Solen's lantern light. Shadow creatures here are stronger, more aggressive, and more numerous than anywhere else.

Solen meets **Wrench**, a dwarf-like mechanist who was trapped in the forges when the Hollow Tide struck. Wrench has survived by jury-rigging old mining equipment into defensive turrets. He's pragmatic, gruff, and deeply afraid but won't admit it. He helps Solen navigate the forge machinery — platforms powered by steam vents, conveyor belts over magma, gear-driven elevators — in exchange for her clearing the shadow creatures from his escape route.

The Cinderforge Depths are the most mechanically challenging world, introducing dash (for crossing lava gaps) and combining it with wall jumping and precise platforming. The environment is hostile: falling into magma is instant death (respawn at checkpoint), steam vents push Solen in unpredictable directions, and conveyor belts force constant movement.

The Forge Hearthstone is deep in the volcanic core, encased in a shell of cooled obsidian-shadow that must be broken by redirecting magma flows. The **Molten Warden**, an enormous humanoid figure made of slag metal and shadow-fire, guards the Hearthstone. It was once the forge's master smith, now twisted beyond recognition. The fight is the hardest mandatory boss in the game, with three phases and environmental hazards.

After defeating the Molten Warden, Solen breaks the obsidian shell and reignites the Forge Hearthstone. The magma returns to its natural orange-gold color. The forges begin to hum again.

**Story Beat 4 — A Reunion:** Wrench, freed from his trapped section of the forge, reveals that he knew Solen's father Edric. They worked the same mine. Wrench was there the day of the collapse and has carried guilt for decades because he was the one who approved the tunnel that caved in. He gives Solen a small carved figurine that Edric had been making for her — a wooden lantern, unfinished, that Edric kept in his pocket. Wrench never found the right time to deliver it. Solen holds the figurine and, for the first time since the game began, cries. Then she puts it in her pack, wipes her face, and heads for the Spire.

### Act V: The Spire Ascent (World 5)

The Spire is a citadel of white stone and crystal that floats above Lumara, anchored by the Spire Hearthstone — the largest and most powerful of the five. It was the seat of the First Keepers and later the headquarters of the Lamplighter Order. Varek has claimed it as his throne.

To reach the Spire, Solen must climb a chain of floating platforms called the Ascent — a vertical gauntlet that tests every ability she has acquired: jumping, wall jumping, dashing, dodging, and navigating in near-darkness, because Varek has surrounded the Spire with a dome of Hollow energy that blocks all external light.

The Spire itself is a dungeon in the traditional sense: a series of interconnected rooms with enemies, traps, puzzles, and mini-bosses. The architecture is grand but corrupted — marble halls stained with shadow, stained-glass windows depicting the First Keepers shattered and rearranged to show Varek's face, and the Lamplighter Order's banners torn and replaced with dark cloth.

Solen fights through the Spire's lower levels and encounters Shade again. This time, Shade fights her directly. The battle is intense but Solen prevails. As Shade collapses, his mask breaks, and Solen recognizes him — he was **Aldric**, a Lamplighter who served with her mother. Dying, Aldric tells Solen that Varek didn't corrupt him; he chose to follow Varek because he believed the Lamplighters had failed the world by depending on the Hearthstones instead of finding a way to live without them. He tells Solen: *"He's not wrong about the chains, Solen. He's just wrong about what happens when you break them."* Aldric dies. Solen takes his Lamplighter badge and adds it to her pack.

At the top of the Spire, Solen confronts Varek — the Hollow King — in the Hearthstone Chamber. The Spire Hearthstone is encased in a massive shell of Hollow energy, and Varek stands before it, calm, almost gentle.

**Story Beat 5 — The Climax:** Varek does not attack immediately. He talks. He tells Solen he knew her mother. He says Maren was the best Lamplighter he ever trained alongside — that she understood, better than anyone, that the Hearthstones were a temporary solution. He says that by reigniting the Hearthstones, Solen is just delaying the inevitable — the Hearthstones will dim again, the Hollow will return, and the next generation will face the same fight. He offers her an alternative: let the Hollow claim Lumara, and he will use its power to reshape the world into something that doesn't need light to survive. No more Hearthstones. No more Lamplighters. No more fear of the dark. Solen asks him: *"And everyone who was turned to stone? My mother?"* Varek pauses. *"They would become part of the new world. Not dead. Changed."* Solen's response: *"Changed isn't alive. I'd rather light a candle every day for the rest of my life than let you blow out the sun."* The fight begins.

## The Central Conflict

The central conflict of Ember Tide is not simply light vs. dark. It is a philosophical disagreement about dependence and freedom. Varek believes that Lumara's reliance on the Hearthstones is a form of imprisonment — the world is shackled to five crystals that could fail at any time, and the people live in perpetual anxiety about the darkness at their borders. He wants to free the world from that fear by removing the thing that causes it: the light itself. If there is no light, there is no darkness to fear. If the Hollow is everywhere, it is nowhere.

Solen's counterargument is not intellectual — it is emotional and practical. She doesn't care about cosmic balance or philosophical freedom. She cares about her mother. She cares about the frozen child in Rimehaven. She cares about the flowers that bloom in Hearthstone light and the songs that NPCs remember. The light may be fragile, but it is real, and the things it nurtures are worth protecting, even if protecting them is hard. Winning, for Solen, means bringing her mother back and giving the world one more chance to figure out a better solution — not Varek's solution, but something that doesn't require erasing everything that already exists.

## The Antagonist: Varek, the Hollow King

### Motivation

Varek is not a cackling villain. He is a man who looked at a broken system and decided that the only fix was demolition. His discovery in the Sunken Brine ruins — that the Hearthstones were locks, not gifts — shattered his faith in the Lamplighter Order and everything it stood for. He felt lied to. He felt that generations of Lamplighters had been maintaining a prison without knowing it, and that the Hollow — whatever it truly was — deserved to be free.

Over the decade since his discovery, Varek has communed with the Hollow and gained power from it. But the Hollow has also changed him. He is thinner, paler, with veins of shadow visible beneath his skin. His eyes glow faintly purple. He speaks with two voices — his own, and a deeper echo that is the Hollow speaking through him. He believes he is still in control. He is not.

Varek believes he is right because he has seen the alternative. He has looked into the Abyssal Sea and seen what the First Keepers feared: a vast, patient intelligence that is not evil but alien. It does not hate the light; it simply does not understand why anyone would choose fragility over permanence. Shadow does not burn out. Shadow does not need fuel. Shadow simply is. Varek has accepted this logic and mistaken it for wisdom.

The tragedy of Varek is that his diagnosis is partially correct: the Hearthstones are imperfect, the world's dependence on them is dangerous, and the Lamplighter Order failed to find a better solution. But his cure is worse than the disease. He cannot see this because the Hollow has been whispering in his mind for ten years, slowly replacing his compassion with cold logic.

## Normal Ending

After defeating Varek, Solen reignites the Spire Hearthstone. The combined light of all five Hearthstones blazes across Lumara in a wave of golden fire. The Hollow Tide recedes, pouring back toward the Abyssal Sea like a dark ocean draining through a crack in the earth. The dome of shadow around the Spire shatters. Sunlight — real sunlight, not Hearthstone light — breaks through the clouds for the first time in months.

A montage plays: the Verdant Hollow blooms with color. The ice in Rimehaven begins to melt, and the frozen child's fingers twitch. The forges of the Cinderforge glow warm orange. Petrified villagers throughout Lumara crack and gasp and stumble back to life, confused but alive.

Solen races down from the Spire, through the Ascent, across the map, back to Cinderhearth. She runs to her doorstep. Maren is there — the stone cracking away from her body, color returning to her skin. Maren blinks, looks at Solen, and sees her holding the lantern staff. She smiles. Solen falls into her arms.

The screen fades to black. Text appears: *"The Hearthstones burn bright. The roads are lit. The Lamplighters walk again."*

Then, a final line: *"But beneath the Abyssal Sea, something patient stirs. The Hollow remembers."*

Credits roll.

## Secret / True Ending

To trigger the true ending, the player must collect all 25 Ember Shards and all 5 Lore Stones before defeating Varek. The Lore Stones are hidden in each world and contain fragments of a First Keeper incantation. The Ember Shards, when combined at a hidden altar in the Spire's basement (accessible only with all 25), form a key that opens the **Chamber of Origin** — a room below the Spire Hearthstone that the First Keepers sealed away.

Inside the Chamber of Origin is the **Lantern of Origin** — the sixth light depicted in the Frostfang mural. It is not a Hearthstone. It is a device built by the First Keepers as a failsafe: a way to permanently sever Lumara from the Abyssal Sea, so that the Hollow could never return. The First Keepers never used it because the cost was enormous: activating the Lantern of Origin would drain all five Hearthstones forever. Lumara would survive, but it would need to learn to generate its own light — through fire, through ingenuity, through the simple human act of lighting candles and building hearths. No more magical crystals. No more dependence. True self-sufficiency.

In the true ending, after defeating Varek, Solen does not simply reignite the Spire Hearthstone. She descends into the Chamber of Origin with the complete incantation from the Lore Stones and activates the Lantern of Origin. A massive beam of white light erupts from beneath the Spire, not upward but downward — into the Abyssal Sea, sealing the crack between Lumara and the void permanently. The Hollow screams — not in pain, but in surprise, as if it never believed the little world above would find the courage to cut the cord.

The Hearthstones go dark. All of them. Lumara is plunged into natural night — not the oppressive dark of the Hollow Tide, but ordinary, starlit darkness. And for the first time in Lumara's history, the stars are visible. They were always there, hidden behind the Hearthstones' glow. The sky is full of them.

The same montage plays, but differently: the Verdant Hollow is lit by moonlight and fireflies. Rimehaven thaws under a sky full of aurora. The forges of Cinderforge are lit by real coal fires, stoked by human hands. Petrified villagers return to life and look up at a sky they've never seen before.

Solen returns to Cinderhearth. Maren is alive, free of stone. She looks at the lantern staff — its Hearthstone crystal is dark, spent. Maren takes the staff, turns it over, and strikes a match. She lights the lantern with an ordinary flame. The small, warm glow fills the doorway.

Maren: *"Hearthstone or matchstick, the job's the same."*

Solen: *"One lantern at a time."*

The screen fades. Text appears: *"The Hearthstones are silent. The Hollow is gone. And Lumara, for the first time, belongs only to itself."*

A final image: Solen and Maren walking down the road at dusk, lighting ordinary lanterns one by one, under a sky blazing with stars.

Credits roll with a different, warmer version of the main theme.

After the credits: a single frame — the wooden lantern figurine Edric carved, now finished by Solen's hand and sitting on the mantelpiece of their home, lit by firelight.
